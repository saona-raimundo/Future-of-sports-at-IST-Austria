A Kilterboard would be an extremely efficient use of space and money. A Kilterboard is a 12'x12' board, with a grid of "standardized" climbing holds. The board can change its angle - to create extremely easy or impossibly difficult problems - perfect for a place like IST with a variety of climbing skills. The route-setting (the process of designing new routes to climb, which is the time-consuming and expensive part of owning a climbing gym) is crowdsourced, due to the standardized set of holds - people from all over the world log onto the app and create, rate, and discuss different routes using a specified set of holds. Last I checked there were about 50,000 routes, of all possible difficulties, on the app.

A kilterboard is relatively cheap (~$10K, I think) and they don't take up much space (the board is 12' x 12', you wouldnt even need to build a new building!), on the scale of building an indoor exercise facility. Bouldering on a Kilterboard is also one of the safest ways to climb. Because of the ability to change the angle, this is extremely inclusive - anyone, from a beginner to an absolute expert can find an appropriate route to climb.
